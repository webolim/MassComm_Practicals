Practical Question 1 (Module 1)

[8 Marks]

Topic: Construction of a Simple and Clear Message (Communication & Message)

Question:

You are travelling to meet your friend in another city by train. Construct an SMS that you will send to your friend informing them about your journey.

Your message must clearly mention:

  • Train name and number
  • Date and time of your arrival
  • Name of the railway station where you will arrive

Then answer the questions that follow.

Part A – Drafting the SMS
(4 Marks)

Write a clear, short SMS to your friend with all the required details.

Part B – Conceptual Questions
(4 Marks)

Q1. On the basis of this exercise, write a simple definition of communication.

(2 marks)

Q2. What are the two main conditions necessary for communication to take place?

(2 marks)

Practical Question 2 (Module 2)

[8 Marks]

Topic: Non‑Verbal Communication in Print (Photographs, Gestures, Expressions)

Question:

From newspapers or magazines, select six photographs which clearly show people communicating through body language, facial expressions, or gestures. Paste them neatly in your practical sheet and answer the questions that follow.

Part A – Collection & Labelling
(4 Marks)

Paste six photographs and write the meaning of each non‑verbal expression or gesture shown.

Part B – Identification Questions
(4 Marks)

Q1. From your six photographs, identify any two where hand movements are used. Describe the meaning of each.

(2 marks)

Q2. From your six photographs, identify any two where facial expressions are mainly used. Describe the emotion shown.

(2 marks)

PART B – CONCEPTUAL QUESTIONS

(4 marks)

Q1. Definition of Communication (2 Marks)

Model Answer: "Process of exchanging information/ideas between sender and receiver through a message/medium."

Marking: 1 mark for "process of sharing/exchanging", 1 mark for "sender/receiver involved".

Q2. Two Main Conditions for Communication (2 Marks)

Model Answer: 1. There must be a sender. 2. There must be a receiver who understands the message.

Marking: 1 mark for Sender, 1 mark for Receiver.

SECTION B: VIVA-VOCE (4 MARKS)

Instructions for Examiner:

  • Ask any 4 questions from the bank below.
  • Each question carries 1 mark.
  • Allow student 1-2 minutes to answer each question.
  • Award marks based on correctness and clarity of response.

Q1. What is a 'message' in communication?

Model Answer: "A message is the information, idea, thought, or feeling that the sender wants to convey to the receiver through some medium."

Marking: 1 mark for defining it as info transferred from sender to receiver. 0.5 if sender-receiver aspect is missing.

Q2. Name any TWO basic elements of the communication process.

Model Answer: Any two: Sender, Receiver, Message, Medium/Channel, Feedback, Noise/Barrier.

Marking: 1 mark for naming any 2 correct elements. 0.5 marks if only 1 is correct.

Q3. Why should a message be short and clear when you send an SMS?

Model Answer: "An SMS has limited character length, and the receiver should quickly understand the important information without any confusion."

Marking: 1 mark for mentioning limited space AND clarity to prevent misunderstanding.

Q4. Give one example of miscommunication caused by an unclear message.

Model Answer: "Sending 'Meeting tomorrow at station' without mentioning which station, causing friends to go to different places."

Marking: 1 mark for a realistic example of confusion. 0.5 marks if example is vague.

Q5. What is non-verbal communication?

Model Answer: "Exchange of information without words, using body language, facial expressions, gestures, posture, etc."

Marking: 1 mark for definition + at least 2 examples (gestures, expressions). 0.5 for definition only.

Q6. Give one example each of a gesture and a facial expression.

Model Answer: Gesture: Thumbs up, waving, pointing. Facial Expression: Smiling, frowning, wide eyes.

Marking: 1 mark for correctly naming one of EACH. 0.5 marks if one is missing/incorrect.

Q7. Can non-verbal communication change the meaning of verbal communication? Give a short example.

Model Answer: "Yes. E.g., saying 'I'm happy' with an angry face/tone changes the meaning to sarcasm or unhappiness."

Marking: 1 mark for 'Yes' + relevant example of contradiction. 0.5 if example is weak.

Q8. Why is it important for a communicator to understand body language?

Model Answer: "It helps to correctly interpret others' true feelings and ensures messages are sent and received effectively."

Marking: 1 mark for explaining it helps interpretation/effectiveness. 0.5 for partial reason.

GENERAL MARKING GUIDELINES

  • Award marks strictly according to the breakup provided.
  • Be generous with alternative but correct answers, especially in Part B.
  • For Viva, ask questions in a conversational tone. Award half marks for partially correct answers.
  • Assessment Scale: 16-20 (Excellent), 12-15 (Good), 8-11 (Satisfactory), < 8 (Needs Improvement).
