Mass Communication (335)

Practical Examination Question Paper

Total Marks: 20

(Distribution: Q1: 8 Marks | Q2: 8 Marks | Viva: 4 Marks)

Time: 3 Hrs

Practical Question 1 (Module 1)

[8 Marks]

Topic: Construction of a Simple and Clear Message (Communication & Message)

Question:

You are travelling to meet your friend in another city by train. Construct an SMS that you will send to your friend informing them about your journey.

Your message must clearly mention:

  • Train name and number
  • Date and time of your arrival
  • Name of the railway station where you will arrive

Then answer the questions that follow.

Part A – Drafting the SMS
(4 Marks)

Write a clear, short SMS to your friend with all the required details.

Part B – Conceptual Questions
(4 Marks)

Q1. On the basis of this exercise, write a simple definition of communication.

(2 marks)

Q2. What are the two main conditions necessary for communication to take place?

(2 marks)

Practical Question 2 (Module 2)

[8 Marks]

Topic: Non‑Verbal Communication in Print (Photographs, Gestures, Expressions)

Question:

From newspapers or magazines, select six photographs which clearly show people communicating through body language, facial expressions, or gestures. Paste them neatly in your practical sheet and answer the questions that follow.

Part A – Collection & Labelling
(4 Marks)

Paste six photographs and write the meaning of each non‑verbal expression or gesture shown.

Part B – Identification Questions
(4 Marks)

Q1. From your six photographs, identify any two where hand movements are used. Describe the meaning of each.

(2 marks)

Q2. From your six photographs, identify any two where facial expressions are mainly used. Describe the emotion shown.

(2 marks)

NIOS Mass Communication (335)

Practical Examination – Modules 1 & 2

MARKING SCHEME & ANSWER KEY

Total Marks: 20 (Practical: 16 + Viva: 4)
Time: 3 Hours

SECTION A: PRACTICAL QUESTIONS (16 MARKS)

Practical Question 1 (Module 1)
[8 Marks]

Topic: Construction of a Simple and Clear Message

PART A – DRAFTING THE SMS

(4 marks)
Component & Criteria Marks
Inclusion of train name and number: Mentions specific train name (e.g., Shatabdi) & number. (0.5 mark if only one present) 1
Date and time of arrival: Clear date and specific time. (0.5 mark if only one present) 1
Station name: Specific railway station name mentioned. 1
Clarity & Structure: Concise, easy to understand, well-structured. 1
TOTAL PART A 4

Model Answer (Example):

"Hi Priya, I'm coming by Shatabdi Express 12027 on 20 January. Arriving at Chennai Central at 6:30 pm. Please pick me up at main entrance. See you soon! – Arjun"

PART B – CONCEPTUAL QUESTIONS

(4 marks)

Q1. Definition of Communication (2 Marks)

Model Answer: "Process of exchanging information/ideas between sender and receiver through a message/medium."

Marking: 1 mark for "process of sharing/exchanging", 1 mark for "sender/receiver involved".

Q2. Two Main Conditions for Communication (2 Marks)

Model Answer: 1. There must be a sender. 2. There must be a receiver who understands the message.

Marking: 1 mark for Sender, 1 mark for Receiver.

Practical Question 2 (Module 2)
[8 Marks]

Topic: Non-Verbal Communication in Print Media

PART A – COLLECTION & LABELLING

(4 marks)
Component & Criteria Marks
Collection: 6 relevant photos showing non-verbal comm. (2 marks for 6 good photos, 1 mark for 4 photos). 2
Explanation: Clear meaning written for each photo. (2 marks for all correct, 1 mark for 4 correct). 2
TOTAL PART A 4

PART B – IDENTIFICATION QUESTIONS

(4 marks)

Q1. Hand Movements (2 Marks)

Criteria: 1 mark for correct photo ID, 1 mark for correct description of meaning.

Examples: Thumbs up (Approval), Pointing finger (Warning/Anger), Handshake (Greeting).

Q2. Facial Expressions (2 Marks)

Criteria: 1 mark for correct photo ID, 1 mark for correct emotion description.

Examples: Smile (Happiness), Wide eyes (Surprise/Shock), Frown (Anger/Sadness).

SECTION B: VIVA-VOCE (4 MARKS)

Instructions for Examiner:

  • Ask any 4 questions from the bank below.
  • Each question carries 1 mark.
  • Award marks based on correctness and clarity.

Q1. What is a 'message'?

Ans: Information/idea sent from sender to receiver.

Q2. Two elements of communication?

Ans: Sender, Receiver, Message, Medium, etc.

Q3. Why SMS short & clear?

Ans: Limited space; avoid misunderstanding.

Q4. Example of miscommunication?

Ans: Vague details causing confusion (e.g., wrong station).

Q5. What is non-verbal comm?

Ans: Communication without words (gestures, expressions).

Q6. Example of Gesture/Face?

Ans: Waving (Gesture), Smiling (Face).

GENERAL MARKING GUIDELINES

  • Award marks strictly according to the breakup provided.
  • Be generous with alternative but correct answers, especially in Part B.
  • For Viva, ask questions in a conversational tone. Award half marks for partially correct answers.
  • Assessment Scale: 16-20 (Excellent), 12-15 (Good), 8-11 (Satisfactory), < 8 (Needs Improvement).
