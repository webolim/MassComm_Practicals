LESSON 1: INTRODUCTION TO COMMUNICATION

üîë KEY CONCEPTS

What is Communication?

Definition Explanation
"Communication is a message understood" Unless a message is understood, communication has NOT taken place
"Communication is social interaction through messages" Society survives through message exchanges
"Communication is sharing of experience" We share feelings/experiences with others

Two Conditions for Communication:

  1. There should be a clear message
  2. The message must be understood by the receiver

üìù WHY DO WE COMMUNICATE?

  1. To ask questions and get answers
  2. To seek and share information
  3. To discuss problems and reach conclusions
  4. To exchange ideas
  5. To interact with others
  6. To understand each other
  7. To solve problems
  8. For survival in society

üëÅÔ∏è FIVE SENSES OF COMMUNICATION

Sense Used For
üëÅÔ∏è Sight Reading, seeing gestures, facial expressions
üëÇ Hearing Listening to speech, sounds
üëÉ Smell Detecting scents, warnings
üëÖ Taste Experiencing flavors
‚úã Touch Physical contact, textures

ü§´ NON-VERBAL COMMUNICATION

Definition: Communication WITHOUT using words

Types:

  • Facial expressions (smile, frown, nod, eye contact)
  • Body movements (waving 'bye', pointing fingers, handshakes)
  • Gestures (NAMASTE - sign of welcome/respect in India, Nepal, Sri Lanka)
Important Fact: Almost 80% of all communication is non-verbal!

üó£Ô∏è ORAL COMMUNICATION

Definition: Communication using spoken language (words and sentences)

‚úÖ Advantages:

  • Spontaneous and natural
  • Easy for others to understand
  • Word choice suits listeners
  • Supported by non-verbal cues
  • Communicator is physically present
  • Can develop close relations

‚ùå Disadvantages:

  • Words disappear into thin air
  • Temporary
  • Often forgotten
  • Non-verbal support may not be understood by other cultures
India's Oral Tradition: Vedas, Puranas, Shastras were passed down orally

‚úçÔ∏è WRITTEN COMMUNICATION

History:

  • Early writing on leather scrolls and palm leaves
  • Paper invented by Chinese and later in Egypt
  • Printing invented by Johannes Gutenberg (15th century)

‚úÖ Advantages:

  • Gives words and thoughts permanence
  • Knowledge available to those who read
  • Led to spread of ideas
  • Creates historical records

‚ùå Disadvantages:

  • One must be literate to use it

üìä TYPES OF COMMUNICATION

Type Definition Example
Intrapersonal Communicating with oneself; looking inward Thinking, self-reflection, batsman talking to himself at crease
Interpersonal One-to-one communication; face to face Conversation between two friends; can be formal or informal
Group Communication People known to each other meet and talk Classroom discussion, canteen conversation, family meeting
Public Communication Many people receive messages from one person Rally speech, public address, lecture

LESSON 6: WHAT IS NEWS?

üîë DEFINITION OF NEWS

News: A development that has happened in the past 24 hours which was not known outside, which is of wide interest to people, and generates curiosity among listeners.

N-E-W-S Mnemonic:

Letter Direction
N North
E East
W West
S South
Meaning: News can come from anywhere!

‚ùì THE 5W + 1H FORMULA

Every perfect news item answers these six questions:

Question What it asks
WHAT? What happened?
WHO? Who is involved?
WHEN? When did it happen?
WHERE? Where did it happen?
WHY? Why did it happen?
HOW? How did it happen?

üìä NEWS vs INFORMATION

News Information
Recent/new development Can be old or new
Of wide public interest May be specific to individuals
Generates curiosity May not generate curiosity
Time-bound (24 hours) Not necessarily time-bound

Example:

  • "Consumer Price Index increased by 2%" = Information
  • "Prices of vegetables doubled overnight due to floods" = News

‚≠ê 10 NEWS VALUES

Value Meaning Example
Timeliness How recent is it? Breaking news
Impact How many people affected? Earthquake affecting thousands
Proximity How close to the reader? Local accident vs. foreign incident
Controversy Is there conflict/debate? Political disputes
Prominence Are famous people involved? Celebrity news
Currency Is it trending? Ongoing elections
Oddity Is it unusual/strange? Man bites dog!
Emotion Does it evoke feelings? Rescue of child from well
Usefulness Is it helpful to readers? Health tips, weather forecast
Educational Value Does it teach something? Scientific discoveries

üì∞ TYPES OF NEWS

Type Description Example
Hard News Serious, time-sensitive, immediate Political events, accidents, disasters
Soft News Lighter, human interest, not urgent Feature stories, lifestyle

Other Types:

  • Features - In-depth stories
  • Profiles - About newsmakers
  • Human Interest - Emotional, personal stories
  • Backgrounders - Context and history

‚úÖ THREE KEY QUALITIES OF NEWS

Quality Meaning How to achieve
Objectivity Without any bias Present facts, not opinions
Fairness Presenting both sides Get all perspectives
Credibility Trustworthiness Cross-check facts before publishing
Cross-checking = Verifying information before printing (essential for credibility)
